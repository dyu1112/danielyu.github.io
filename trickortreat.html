Animalese-esque Dialogue Blips

Inspired by Animal Crossing's fictional animalese language. In Unity, in an OnCharacterTyped() function in conjunction with Yarn Spinner's typewriter effect, Unity would send the character typed and the NPC who spoke it to Wwise through Wwise Switches. Using that information, Wwise would retrieve the sound for that character corresponding to that NPC and play it, creating the illusion of animalese.

To support the player's objective in differentiating between monsters and kids, in Wwise, monster dialogue blips would be pitched to be lower and less fluctuating to sound less humanlike while kid dialogue blips would fluctuate in pitch in a higher range.
